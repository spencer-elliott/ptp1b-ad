Processing the raw data involves huge files and way more processing power than I have on my laptop.
I'll start by downloading just one SRA run in fastq format:
SRR36691286 (C57BL/6; APP/PS1; PTP1B-/-)
I downloaded this using SRAtoolkit and compressed it into a gzip right away.

Next I ran FastQC to check out the quality of our raw data. Looks good, there are some sections flagged in each report
but looking at these they are not serious concerns. The sequences have a high quality score throughout.

Conda environment for fastQC:
conda create -n rnaseq fastqc sra-tools -c bioconda -c conda-forge
conda activate rnaseq

kallisto bus

Conda environment for kallisto bus:
conda create -n kallisto_env -c conda-forge -c bioconda kallisto bustools
conda activate kallisto_env

I found the whitelist for the 10x genomics platform online:
curl -O mereu2020/10xV3 wget https://teichlab.github.io/scg_lib_structs/data/10X-Genomics/3M-february-2018.txt.gz
gunzip mereu2020/10xV3/3M-february-2018.txt.gz

Generating transcripts to genes file:
gunzip gencode.vM23.annotation.gtf.gz
awk '$3 == "transcript"' gencode.vM23.annotation.gtf | \
awk '{print $10"\t"$14}' | tr -d '";' > transcripts_to_genes.txt
